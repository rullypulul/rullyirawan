Kapauhagen, Logotype, 2024, Copenhagen

I have a chat group with my Indoneian friends that studied art together back home. When my daughter was born one of them wrote I should put "Kapauhagen" in her name mixing the word 'Kapau' (Kapau is a specialty food stands from West Sumatra where the roots of my family came from.) and Copenhagen. He wrote it as a joke, a funny one according to the group standards. I decided to visualise it using cliché and exaggerated characteristics from both cultures.

Graphic design graduation show, Identity, 2019, Netherlands

I was working alongside several students guided by Agata Jaworska and ina Hollman to design the identity of our department’s graduation show. Aside from curating the student’s projects, i got the responsibil- ity to design 50 creatures that represented each graduation project. We sent questionnaires to each student so we can categorize and make a mapping and placement of each project, also as a guideline for me to design the creatures. These creatures were applied as animation in the crowdfunding video, window stickers, printed on merchandise ( i.e. tee’s and keychains), on the website and in the booklet.

Migrare, Documentary film, 2019, Netherlands

Filmed from God’s point of view, Migrare is a documentary featuring undocumented people from indonesia living inside the social structures of The Netherlands. This interview based documentary is an attempt to reveal the humanistic side of these migrants, giving the audience insight into what it is like to live without legality–what this means to them and how they see themselves inside the “illegal immigrants” stigma. Film- ing this movie from above made me able to hide the interviewees identity without using the traditional “blurred face” or “black square censor” on their faces that are often applied to interview criminals or crime witnesses. it also put the viewer as more as an observer of how these undocumented migrants live in the Netherlands. i learned to film with a drone.

City Displacement Tour, Identity, 2017, Netherlands

City Displacement’ is one of the five public programs presented within the ‘Mentality-No style’ project in the context of the 100 Years of the De stijl/Mondrian year event. A project organized by a group of students of the Royal Academy of Art together with the municipality of The Hague. i was working together with classmate Peter Van Langen on this particular project. inspired by the mentality of of De stijl movement and Agitation Propaganda Tour, we initiated a bus tour event around The Hague. We researched for signs of gentrification in these neighborhoods and chose six designated spots. in the bus we invited some activists to discuss and share their knowledge about gentrification. For the project, we designed posters, flyers, flags, and social media posters. We went to The Hague image library gathering images of old and recent images to use as our visual language to show transformations in these areas.

Hazyland, Documentary film and photo book, 2020, Netherlands

Hazyland brings together the voices of fellows and experts around the theme of indorock, a music genre created in the Netherlands in the rock and roll era of the 1950s. The interviews with Woody brunings, Leon Donnars and Harm Peter smilde focus in particular on the creative work of the Tielman brothers, who are seen as the pioneers of indorock. The video critically discusses the use of terminology rooted in ethnicity such as ‘indorock’ questioning whether such naming perpetuates racism.
